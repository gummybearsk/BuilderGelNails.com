## How to Pick the Right Kit

- **If you want pro feel fast:** Kokoist or The GelBottle BIAB for stiffer brushes and crisp apex control.

## Key Mistakes to Avoid
- Pairing budget gels with underpowered lamps; give them 60–120s under 48W+.
- Skipping base coat because the bottle says “builder”; base still improves adhesion.
- Applying BIAB thick like acrylic; use thin layers and a controlled apex.
- Ignoring sensitivity signs; switch to HEMA-free Au Lait and keep gel off skin if any redness appears.
- Not capping the free edge; beginners often miss this and see early chips.

## FAQs

**Do these kits include lamps?**
Most don’t. Use a 48W+ LED lamp; weaker lamps cause under-curing and lifting.

**Can I use them on natural nails only?**
Yes—keep nails short, build a small apex, and infill every 2–3 weeks.

**What top coat should I use?**
A no-wipe top coat is easiest. Ensure it’s compatible with your builder gel brand to avoid wrinkling.

**How do I avoid lifting with budget kits?**
Meticulous prep, thin base, keep off skin, and extend cure to 120s on thumbs. Lifting is usually prep- or cure-related, not brand-related.

## Final Expert Advice (Sara Kim)
Match the kit to your speed and sensitivity. Start with forgiving, self-leveling formulas, then graduate to stiffer pro options as your apex control improves. Whatever you buy, give it a strong lamp, a clean prep, and patient cures—that’s what makes builder gel nails last.
